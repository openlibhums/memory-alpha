## Timeline 

2012 – **Literature scholar Caroline Edwards starts a publishing project** using WordPress at Birkbeck, University of London. Friend and Birkbeck colleague Martin Eve offers to help with HTML.
 
2013 – **Caroline and Martin found the Open Library of Humanities (OLH)**, while carrying on full careers as Birkbeck academics. They start publishing OLH journals on Open Journal Systems, through a private hosting provider. 
 
2014 – **Martin publishes Open Access and the Humanities: Contexts, Controversies, and the Future (Cambridge University Press, 2014).** It delves into the history and promise of open access humanities publishing in the humanities. 
 
2017? – **Publishing technology expert Andy Byers joins the OLH.** The precursor to Janeway is hammered out over one long weekend of coding. To keep the OLH from being locked into a vendor’s profit model, Martin and Andy host it under the copyleft Affero General Public License (AGPL) 3.0 software license. 
 
2018 - **Andy launches Janeway migration and hosting services.** At the Library Publishing Forum at the University of Minnesota, Janeway opens to other nonprofit publishers.
 
2019 - **Library publishers sign up for hosting services**, and the team rapidly build out new features to meet the varying needs of the new clients. 
 
2021 - **Arcadia awards Birkbeck a £200,000 grant** which funds further development of the Janeway platform. 
 
2021 – **The OLH’s previous host is bought out by a larger for-profit publisher.** But the OLH finishes migrating all its journals to Janeway in the nick of time. 
 
2022 – **A working group integrates UX research into the design process.** The Janeway team experiments with a shift towards human-centred design. 
 
2023 - **Birkbeck invest into a five-year sustainability plan.** They approve expansion of the team to build greater support, outreach, research and development capacity. 
 
2023 - **Janeway sets up a governance board** to be accountable to the community of librarians, editors, and open source developers. 
 
2024 – **New staff focus on an Accessibility Roadmap,** to make the documentation and interface usable for all.
